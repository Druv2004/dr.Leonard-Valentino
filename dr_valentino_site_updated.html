Professional Overview

Physician. Researcher. Educator.

Dr. Leonard Valentino is a translational hematologist and health equity advocate with over 30 years of experience spanning academic medicine, biotechnology, and nonprofit leadership. He is a recognized leader in hemophilia research, drug development, and medical affairs.

He currently serves as President of the World Federation of Hemophilia USA and as Entrepreneur in Residence at Pathway to Cures — a mission-driven investment model advancing innovation across inheritable blood and bleeding disorders. He was previously Chief Executive Officer of the National Bleeding Disorders Foundation and VP of Strategy at Spark Therapeutics.

Biography

Academic & Professional Journey

Dr. Leonard Valentino is an experienced translational hematologist with a demonstrated history of strategic development across academia, biotechnology, and the nonprofit sector. He received his medical degree from Creighton University and completed his residency at the University of Illinois College of Medicine, followed by a Pediatric Hematology-Oncology Fellowship at the David Geffen School of Medicine at UCLA (1987–1990).

He is board-certified by the American Board of Pediatrics and the American Board of Pediatric Hematology/Oncology. Over his 30+ year career, Dr. Valentino has made significant contributions to the understanding and treatment of hemophilia, von Willebrand disease, and rare bleeding disorders — spanning clinical research, drug development, and medical affairs roles in both industry and academia.

He served as Chief Executive Officer of the National Bleeding Disorders Foundation from 2020 to 2024, and as VP of Strategy Lead for Hematology at Spark Therapeutics. He currently leads the World Federation of Hemophilia USA as President and serves as Entrepreneur in Residence at Pathway to Cures, championing innovation and health equity for people living with blood disorders.
